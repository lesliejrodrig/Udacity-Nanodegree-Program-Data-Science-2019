Now that I have loaded the dataset, I am ready to clean the data
Here is a quick summary of some of the cleaning I will do on the data:

  1. Check for records that have missing userId
  2. Check for records that have null sessionId
  3. Check for records that have missing gender inputs
  4. Check distribution of columns to remove any possible columns that may not have variation (single valued columns)
  5. Convert ts to proper timestamp format
  6. Convert registration to proper timestamp format
  7. Split location column into two new columns - userCity, and userState
  8. Remove userAgent, method, location column as they do not appear to be relevant for my analysis

Since the churned users are a fairly small subset, I suggest using F1 score as the metric to optimize.

For this step of my project, I will be building 2 base models that could possibly be used for predicting churn. Since churn is a simple churned/not-churned value, I can leverage classification models. The models I will be building are below:
